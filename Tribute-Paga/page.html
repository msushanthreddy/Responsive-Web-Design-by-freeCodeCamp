Norah Mary Vincent

A Trailblazing Journalist and Author

About Norah Vincent

Norah Vincent was an American journalist and author known for her groundbreaking work in exploring gender identity and societal norms. Her book, Self-Made Man, chronicles her 18-month experience living as a man to understand male life and societal expectations.

Early Life and Education

Born on September 20, 1968, in Detroit, Michigan, Vincent pursued philosophy at Williams College, where she honed her analytical skills that would later define her journalistic work.

Career Highlights

Vincent wrote for numerous prestigious publications, including The Los Angeles Times, The New York Times, and The Village Voice. Her work often tackled issues related to gender, identity, and social norms with insight and wit.

"Self-Made Man"

Published in 2006, Self-Made Man is Vincent's most renowned work. It provides a candid exploration of gender identity through her experiences living as "Ned." Her journey highlights the pressures men face and challenges societal gender norms.

Legacy

Vincent's work continues to inspire conversations about gender and identity, encouraging empathy and understanding across societal divides. She passed away on July 6, 2022, leaving behind a legacy of courage and insight.
